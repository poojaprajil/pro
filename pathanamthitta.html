PATHANAMTHITTA

Pathanamthitta district is known as the 'Headquarters of Pilgrimage Tourism', of the Kerala State. Situated near the Western Ghats and bordered by the hills, Pathanamthitta district is a treat to eyes with its vast unending stretches of forests, rivers and rural landscapes. Blessed by nature, the district is famous for its scenic beauty, fairs and festivals.


Gavi

Gavi is situated some 14 km away from the famed Periyar Tiger Reserve and is heaven on Earth, quite literally.

Triveni Sangamam

As the name suggests this place is famous for the meeting point of the three rivers. It serves as the halting point for those on their way to Sabarimala.

Perunthenaruvi Waterfalls

A natural waterfall with captivating surroundings is situated on the banks of the River Pamba. The fall is 60 to 100 ft deep which pours down on a rocky bed.

Kaviyoor Rock Temple

If you are looking forward to explore the fascinating culture of Kerala, you are at right place. Kaviyoor Rock Temple is the only cave temple left in the state which glorifies the culture.

Muloor Smarakam

Muloor Smarakam is a memorial dedicated to a poet and a social reformer Muloor S Padmanabha Panicker.Situated at a small distance of 12 km from Pathanamthitta town at a small village Elavumthitta.

Sabarimala

One of the major religious hubs of South India, Sabarimala houses Sastha Temple situated about 80 km from Pathanamthitta town. Dedicated to Lord Ayyappa.
