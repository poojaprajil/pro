KANNUR

Kannur is one among the few noteworthy cities in Kerala. Formerly known as Cannanore.The naturally rich land of Kannur has played a major role in contributing to the cultural, religious, political and industrial heritage of Kerala. With several cultural institutions, ancient forts and old shrines, the city of Kannur displays remarkable history. Some of the tourist attractions in Kannur are St. Angelo Fort, Payyambalam Beach, Arrakal Museum, Azhikal Ferry and Aralam Wildlife Sanctuary.


Payyambalam Beach

The Payyambalam Beach is an impeccably maintained destination in Kannur.. Surfing and swimming options are also available for those who seek a little adventure.

Muzzhapilangad Beach

The Muzzhapilangad Drive-in Beach at Kannur is one referred to with many superlatives- as the longest drive-in beach in the whole of India.

Kannur Lighthouse

Being Kerala's first lighthouse, Kannur Lighthouse is a pretty big deal. This cylindrical concrete tower has a balcony with a light that is lit as the evening breaks in to alert the seamen of the land.

Kannur snake park

Kannur snake park is one of the most prominent reptile preservation centres in the country and is the only one in Kerala. It is home to over 150 different species of reptiles.

Arakkal Museum

Arakkal Museum celebrates the memory and grandeur of Kerala's only Muslim royal family- the Arakkal Ali Rajas. This museum is a testimony to the impeccable Muslim architecture and design.

Theyyam

One of the most celebrated and famed ritual art form in Kerala is Theyyam.Originated in North Kerala,Theyyam brings to life the great stories of our State.It encompasses dance, mime and music.
